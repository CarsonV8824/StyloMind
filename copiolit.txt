The night settled over the city like a soft, dark blanket, muffling the usual hum of traffic until only the distant echo of a train remained. In a quiet apartment above a closed café, a single lamp glowed against the window. Inside, a young writer stared at a blank page, waiting for inspiration to arrive. But inspiration had its own ideas. A faint breeze stirred the papers on the desk, though the windows were shut. The writer looked up, confused, as a line of text slowly appeared on the page—written in handwriting that wasn’t theirs.
